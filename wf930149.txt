ECONOMY 
Overview: The economy is based largely on tourism (including gambling) 
and textile and fireworks manufacturing. Efforts to diversify have spawned 
other small industries - toys, artificial flowers, and electronics. The 
tourist sector has accounted for roughly 25% of GDP, and the clothing 
industry has provided about two-thirds of export earnings; the gambling 
industry represented 36% of GDP in 1991. Macau depends on China for most of 
its food, fresh water, and energy imports. Japan and Hong Kong are the main 
suppliers of raw materials and capital goods.

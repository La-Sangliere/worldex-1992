ECONOMY 
Overview: The Gambia has no important mineral or other natural resources 
and has a limited agricultural base. It is one of the world's poorest 
countries with a per capita income of about $230. About 75% of the 
population is engaged in crop production and livestock raising, which 
contribute 30% to GDP.  Small-scale manufacturing activity - processing 
peanuts, fish, and hides - accounts for less than 10% of GDP. Tourism is a 
growing industry. The Gambia imports one-third of its food, all fuel, and 
most manufactured goods. Exports are concentrated on peanut products (about 
75% of total value).
